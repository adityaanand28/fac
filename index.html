About Us

Finance and Analytics Club Club aims to spread financial literacy amongst the campus community & aspires to make everyone adept in handling Personal Finances. We work at the merger of finance and analytics applying analytical understandings and tools in the areas of finance and economics. In the era of digitalization, rapid growth opportunities in fintech industries have sprung. Triumphing for these opportunities requires relevant skills and domain knowledge, which ranges from understanding basic financial statements to capital markets, Corporate Finance, Econometrics, art of investing, building trading ideas, strategies and FAC (IITK) feels humbled in covering these.

FAC (IITK) timetable is circumscribed with engaging activities throughout the year including lectures, presentations, summer projects, assignments, workshops, seminars on certifications, guest speakers & Q&A sessions.

WE BELIEVE IN GREAT IDEAS

We believe that an investment in learning pays the best interest and that one’s degree should never become an impediment in their way to pursue the things they are passionate about. So, we organize various events, workshops and quizzes for the campus community curious about finance and analytics.

Apart from this, we offer a multitude of projects that touch upon different aspects. We believe this would promote a holistic development of our own as well as other interested students’ knowledge of finance.

We also try to keep everyone updated with what is going on in the corporate world by posting business and finance related articles regularly on our Facebook handle.

    Ongoing Projects

"“Know what you own and why you own it” - Peter Lynch Finance is an inseparable part of everyone’s life.We noticed that opportunities in the field of finance were very limited. We noticed fewer corporates visiting our campus for finance roles compared to other IITs. So I pitched the idea of a focused club/hobby group to Simrat. A club which shall serve a dual purpose - grooming the talent pool for finance-related opportunities and increasing financial literacy of the campus junta. It was our final year with placements close but we decided to give it a try. We took the idea to Senate .Finally, after much deliberations, we got a green signal.We planned and worked rigorously. The response that we received from the campus junta was overwhelming. Post the placement season, our focus shifted towards improving financial literacy. We offered students to be a part of a semester-long boot camp and ensured that performing students were given adequate credentials/certificates which would help them in the future internship/placement process. We tried to give our best to the campus junta . And so the club’s legacy continues The club gives you a great head start to build a career in finance. Overall, the learning experience was great !!"

Anish Lamba Ex - Coordinator Finance Discussion Group

"I started with FDG in the second year of its existence when I met Anish and Simrat(Founders) and I was asked to lead the group. With the vision to help more students in not only decoding the financial industry but also help them in preparing for finance roles, I started my journey. We organised topic specific lectures, competitons in Takneek, winter projects camp where we mentored students on topics like Derivative Pricing Models, Portfolio Optimization etc, case study event in Techkriti and a trading/investing course in Partnership with a professional platform. It was an amazing learning experience for me. Being a management student, I didn’t had much interactions with SnT Council but to be honest, everyone was extremely supportive. I am sure the club is going to touch greater heights in the years to come."

Rishabh Gupta Ex - Coordinator Finance Discussion Group

"As I joined IITK, I was amazed by the overwhelming variety of student activities on campus but it took me some time to realize there was still scope for improvement. Being from an Economics background, a proclivity towards finance was natural, and not much to my surprise Finance Discussion Group existed on campus. My first interaction with the Hobby Group was through an innovative certificate-based lecture series. My tenure started at an irregular time and, I wanted to convert the group to a club with much-more reach on campus. We started increasing the reach through sessions, workshops, newsletters, projects, and whatnot. At the end of my tenure both at IITK and hobby-group, we managed to organize a Non-Core Weekend with eminent personalities as speakers. Finally with the help of Gensec and my fellow-coordinators we were able to convert the FDG hobby-group into Finance and Analytics Club (FAC). This whole duration taught me a lot more than just finance, public-speaking, managing events. I owe a lot to the club itself, but it is still a lot more to go. This experience impacted and changed my personality for good."

Prajual Maheshwari Ex - Coordinator ,Finance Discussion Group
