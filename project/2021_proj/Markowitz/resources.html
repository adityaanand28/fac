    • Assignment

  • Assignment 1
    • Those who haven't taken up HSO201/MSO201 (or any course on Probability & Statistics), you're first expected to go through the concept of Volatility/ Standard Deviation.
    1. Read up about Volatility of a Stock price series. It is used as a proxy for the risk associated with a stock. In simple terms. the more variable/fluctuating the time series data is, the higher is the risk of holding onto that investment. (You have more chance of losing big bucks if the price of a stock falls, say, 20% in a single day)
    2. CAGR (Compounded Annual Growth Rate) represents the annual growth of an investment over a particular time period. (In simple terms: If you've held onto the stock for 3 years, then the amount you've earned every year)
    3. Sharpe Ratio: Risk-Adjusted Return. Excess return that an investment earns relative to the risk it carries with itself. (aim is to look for stocks having high returns with low risk)
    Task: Define 3 functions to calculate Volatility, CAGR and Sharpe Ratio of a stock price series. In the calculation of Sharpe Ratio, take the Expected Portfolio Return = CAGR & Risk-free rate = 6% (0.06). Data Set: NIFTY 50 stocks (Time Period: 01/01/2016 - 01/01/2020) Identify the top 15 stocks having the highest Sharpe Ratio.

    • 1. The attached Python code also contains the correct answers to the Previous Assignment. You're all supposed to use the 15 stocks obtained in the current Python file and use them further. (Run it on your pc first, reading the data of nifty50 csv I shared with you earlier)
    2. Calculate Log Returns of the 15 stocks
    3. Initialize random weights (A very large number ~600000 is taken) to have a good representative sample to choose from
    4. Obtain 600000 random set of weights for 15 stocks, the expected return, volatility & sharpe ratio of the portfolio corresponding to those weights.
    5. Identify the Max Sharpe Ratio of the Portfolio from the random weights & Plot the return & volatility of the portfolios on a Scatter Plot
    6. Plot the Efficient Frontier using SLSQP method (already coded)
    7. Total amt = 1,00,00,000. Obtain the Amount of Money Invested in each stock & the number of shares held of each (on the last testing day). We'll test the weights and trade them for the months in 2021.

  • Assignment 3
    • In the previous assignment, we have obtained optimal weights ( weights corresponding to Maximum Sharpe Ratio) for 15 stocks & obtained Efficient Frontier as well. We divided our total money according to those weights & bought a corresponding number of shares for each stock. For now, our portfolio contains 15 stocks.
    In this assignment, we need to trade the portfolio by holding it over the Testing Period.
    Metrics to evaluate Portfolio:
    1) Total Return on Portfolio
    2) Volatility
    3) Beta
    4) Sharpe Ratio
    5) Jenson's Alpha
    6) Sortino Ratio
    7) Treynor's Ratio
    You need to compare all these metrics wrt the Benchmark i.e. Nifty50 & report all the results.
    Now, we'll go one-step further to better our Portfolio. We'll observe that these 15 stock portfolio doesn't give good returns as what one should expect. This might be because 15 stocks is a large number to have in a portfolio. (You all are encouraged to think of other ways to improve the performance, for now, you are supposed to the complete the below task)
    We've read that diversifying our portfolio reduces risk (having uncorrelated stocks in a portfolio is better from a risk-perspective)
    What you need to do now is create a correlation matrix (using .corr() function ) for the 15 stock prices. This will result in 15*15 matrix. We need to identify a group of 4 such stocks which have the minimum correlation (i.e. corr(1,2) + corr(1,3) + corr(1,4) + corr(2,3) + corr(2,4) + corr(3,4) is minimum).
    After identifying 4 stocks, we'll repeat the same process, obtain optimal weights for Markowitz portfolio, number of shares to be held in the testing period & compare the metrics wrt the Benchmark performance.
