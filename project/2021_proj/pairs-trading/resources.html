    • What is OHLC data?
    OHLC stands for Open High Low Close
    Open price for an interval refers to the stock price at the starting of the interval. So if we consider the time interval [t1,t2], then the open price is the stock for this interval is the price of the stock at time t1.
    Close price for an interval refers to the stock price at the end of the interval. So if we consider the time interval [t1,t2], then the close price is the stock for this interval is the price of the stock at time t2.
    Low price for an interval refers to the lowest price that the stock goes for an interval. So if we consider the time interval [t1,t2], then the low price is the stock for this interval is the minimum price of the stock in the time interval [t1,t2].
    High price for an interval refers to the highest price that the stock goes for an interval. So if we consider the time interval [t1,t2], then the high price is the stock for this interval is the maximum price of the stock in the time interval [t1,t2].
    Lets say we have the stock price from 10:11 am to 10:20 am varying as follows ( we assume that the stock price change every minute)
    10:11 am - Rs. 338
    10:12 am - Rs. 340
    10:13 am - Rs. 343
    10:14 am - Rs. 339
    10:15 am - Rs. 325
    10:16 am - Rs. 328
    10:17 am - Rs. 334
    10:18 am - Rs. 328
    10:19 am - Rs. 340
    10:20 am - Rs. 342
    So for the time interval [10:11 am , 10:20 am] we have
    Open price = Rs. 338
    Close Price = Rs. 342
    High Price = Rs. 343
    Low Price = Rs. 325

    • Hello everyone,
    Now we have the list of similar stocks and now we want to learn how to do the trade on those stocks. For trading we can apply various methods, few of which are:
    Use the spread around the mean to calculate when to do the trade
    Using some of the technical indicators like moving averages to find out the trading signals.
    So we will try out both and see which one works better and gives result.
    For the spread in the distance approach , we will use the standard deviation . In this method we will use the following strategy if the distance(Y-X) between the time series at any point becomes such that it is greater than the mean+c_1*standard deviation we will short the stock Y and go for long X and sell whenever the distance becomes less than , mean+ c_2*standard deviation . c is a constant here . Try experimenting with different values of c_1,c_2 (which is a constant here ) , like c_1=1, c_1=0.5 ,etc. Note c_1>c_2 If the distance becomes less than the mean - c*standard deviation , we will follow the reverse strategy .
    For cointegration approach , we can fit a linear regression line for the stock prices(remember for the cointegration to exists between 2 time series X,Y the formula we check is if we can find a good beta such that Y_t = alpha + beta * X_t +error , alpha and beta are constants ) and then calculate the spread form that error. After this consider alpha as the mean of distance between the stocks(one of them is time series Y and the other one is scaled by beta , so the other time series is beta*X ) and error will be used to calculate the standard deviation and follow the similar strategy as in the method 1.
    Now for going through the technical indicators approach you should first learn a bit about some. Here are a few blog to read about the same https://zerodha.com/varsity/chapter/moving-averages/ and then learn how to code it for a single stock , also the medium blog shared earlier(https://medium.com/auquan/pairs-trading-data-science-7dbedafcfe5a) also uses this and has some implementation too. For this we will use the ratio of the stock price to make it a single time series(lets say we change it to Y/X ) and change the trading strategy as follows(read first about the moving averages before reading this) :
    a. When we have to short the price according to the moving averages indicator , this means the time series is above the moving average , so in our series (Y/X) is above it's mean , so we should short on stock Y and go for long on stock X . And whenever the square off signal comes from the moving average for the ratio time series we do the the square of for the stock X and Y.
    b. Reverse happens in the other case.
    Note the technical indicator is same for the distance and cointegration approach
